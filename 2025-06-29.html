Russian metal band's appearance at Tuska Festival draws protests

https://yle.fi/a/74-20170152

2025-06-29

Russian deathcore band Slaughter to Prevail's appearance at the Tuska Festival in Helsinki sparked both an official protest and informal demonstrations during their performance. Demonstrators held signs questioning whether Tuska was the right platform for "Russian propaganda" and asking why the festival was giving space to "Russian patriots" The band previously performed in Washington DC under the Russian flag. No Russian flags or pro-Russian propaganda appeared during Slaughter toPrevail's performance.

Frustration grows among airport workers as ground services labour dispute drags on

https://yle.fi/a/74-20170132

2025-06-29

Industrial dispute affecting ground handling services has polarised opinion among employees. Some express fatigue and frustration over continued strike action. The IAU has staged several four-hour work stoppages in recent weeks, resulting in around a thousand flight cancellations. Mediation in the dispute is scheduled to continue on Sunday. If no agreement is reached, further strikes are planned for Wednesday and Friday, Finnair has warned.

Thunderstorm hits Helsinki as heavy rain and winds sweep southern Finland

https://yle.fi/a/74-20170122

2025-06-29

Storm front passes over Helsinki early Sunday morning. Lightning strikes cause several fires in Satakunta and Pirkanmaa. Localised rainfall could reach up to 40mm, and wind gusts may hit speeds of 20 metres per second. Tuska metal music festival in Helsinki warned that gusty winds could impact Sunday's programme and logistics. Later in the week, low pressure systems are expected to return, bringing cooler and unsettled weather across Finland.
