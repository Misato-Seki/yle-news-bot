FMI: Winter's last ice melts in the Baltic Sea

https://yle.fi/a/74-20164096

2025-05-26

The last fragile floes in the northern Bay of Bothnia have melted. The Gulf of Finland lost its ice cover unusually early this year, becoming ice-free in early April. The last time this happened earlier was in 2020, the Finnish Meteorological Institute said. The largest extent of Baltic Sea ice in recent years occurred in 2011, when over 309,000 square kilometres of the sea froze over.

Finland stocks coastal waters with millions of fish to shore up populations

https://yle.fi/a/74-20164057

2025-05-26

Millions of young fish have been released into rivers, lakes and coastal waters across southern and western Finland as part of a major spring stocking effort. The aim of the stocking programme is to strengthen sustainable fish populations. Anglers are encouraged to report tagged fish from their catches to the Natural Resources Institute Finland (Luke) to support ongoing research. It is funded by fisheries fees collected from operators, which are used to offset the negative impacts on fish stocks.
