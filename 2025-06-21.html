Violence, drink-driving and disorder keep police busy on Midsummer Eve

https://yle.fi/a/74-20168935

2025-06-21

Police across Finland were kept busy on Midsummer Eve with numerous cases involving drink-driving, boating under the influence, assaults and other forms of disorderly conduct. The most serious incidents included two suspected attempted homicides under investigation in Salo and Oulu. In Korsnäs, a detached house caught fire, while in Alajärvi, a search was launched for young paddleboarders who had gone missing.

Electricity spot prices go negative on Midsummer Day

https://yle.fi/a/74-20168921

2025-06-21

The price of electricity on the exchange fell into negative territory for the first time at eleven in the morning. The price will remain negative until 4pm. Saturday spot prices were at their most expensive at 1am when they reached 4.27 cents per kilowatt hour. The lowest price is at 2 pm when the price is minus one cent per kilo-watt-hour (kWh)

Midsummer Day in Finland coincides with summer solstice

https://yle.fi/a/74-20168907

2025-06-21

On Saturday, Finland enjoys more daylight than on any other day of the year. In Helsinki, the sun rose at 3:54am and sets at 10:49pm. From Sunday onwards, daylight hours will gradually begin to shorten again. A band of weather systems is moving southeastwards on Saturday. Across southern Finland it will rain during the day. Sunday will be mostly clear with showers mainly in western Lapland.
