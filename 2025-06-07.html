Gusty winds delay festival openings in southwest Finland

https://yle.fi/a/74-20166383

2025-06-07

Two music festivals forced to delay their openings on Saturday due to strong gusty winds. Gusts of up to 21 metres a second recorded in the Åland islands, west of Turku. The Saaristo festival in Kaarina opened at 2pm, half an hour later than planned. The Kesärauha Festival in Turku was set to start four and a half hours behind schedule.

Pro-Palestine group claims responsibility for paint splattering at Finnish Defence Ministry

https://yle.fi/a/74-20166365

2025-06-07

Main entrance to the Ministry of Defence headquarters was defaced with pink paint in Helsinki's Kaartinkaupunki district early Saturday. Police received a report of vandalism at 1:42 am. Security guard told police that a man and a woman had been observed leaving the scene. Cleaning work at the entrance will start on Monday, said Lieutenant ColonelMikko Koskimaa, military assistant to the ministry’s permanent secretary.

Greens leader Virta re-elected, slams government budget cuts

https://yle.fi/a/74-20166348

2025-06-07

Sofia Virtahas re-elected as chair of the opposition Greens for a second term. She ran unopposed at this weekend’s party conference in Hämeenlinna. In her policy address on Saturday, Virta criticised the current government's budget cuts. She said the Greens want to cut corporate subsidies that are harmful to the environment and prop up current structures.
