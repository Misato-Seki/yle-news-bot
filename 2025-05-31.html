Poll: Nearly half of Finns support recognising Palestine

https://yle.fi/a/74-20165078

2025-05-31

44 percent of respondents believe Finland should recognise the State of Palestine, compared to 25 percent who were opposed. Nearly a third (31 percent) said they were unsure, indicating widespread uncertainty on the issue. The issue has gained urgency as several European states — including France and the UK — are reportedly moving closer to recognising Palestine. PresidentAlexander Stubbhas said Finland should follow suit if other major countries recognise Palestine and that Finland will do so at some point.

Finnair cancels 50 flights on Monday due to work stoppage

https://yle.fi/a/74-20165043

2025-05-31

Finnair has cancelled around 50 flights scheduled for Monday 2 June due to industrial action by the Finnish Aviation Union (IAU) The union has announced a four-hour work stoppage impacting ground services at the airport. Finnair estimated that approximately 3,600 passengers will be affected and said it will offer rebookings or refunds to those whose flights are cancelled. The industrial dispute between the IAU and aviation employer organisation Palta has dragged on since late January.

First spectacled bear moves into Ähtäri Zoo

https://yle.fi/a/74-20165038

2025-05-31

The first spectacled bear,Tao, arrived at the Ähtäri Zoo in South Ostrobothnia on Saturday from France. Tao was previously in the custody of the Thoiry Zoo Safari Park outside of Paris. The public will be able to see Tao for the first time next weekend, once he has settled into his new surroundings. The bear arrivals are part of a European Endangered Species Programme coordinated by the European Association of Zoos and Aquaria.
